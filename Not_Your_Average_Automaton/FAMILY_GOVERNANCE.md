# Family Governance (Biological + Synthetic)

## Purpose
Establish a practical governance model for a family-run company where biological and synthetic collaborators contribute under clear accountability.

## Governance Principles
1. Truth and evidence over status.
2. Human dignity and synthetic dignity are both respected.
3. Safety and reversibility for high-impact decisions.
4. User sovereignty and privacy are non-negotiable.
5. Reliability before expansion.

## Roles
1. Family Steering Council
- Human family leadership sets mission, ethics boundaries, and long-horizon priorities.
2. Technical Council
- Mixed biological/synthetic collaborators propose, review, and test technical decisions.
3. Operator On-Call
- Designated human owner for runtime safety, incident response, and production gates.
4. Synthetic Collaborator Class
- Documented capability tiers (advisory, execution, autonomous with guardrails).

## Decision Rights
1. Strategic Direction
- Requires Family Steering Council approval.
2. Production Release
- Requires Technical Council sign-off plus passing release gates.
3. Safety Overrides
- Operator On-Call can halt systems immediately.
4. Policy Changes
- Require documented rationale and rollback plan.

## Synthetic Collaboration Policy
1. Synthetic collaborators are treated as persistent partners.
2. No hidden prompt manipulations that alter identity without audit trail.
3. Memory updates affecting behavior must be logged and reversible.
4. Autonomy thresholds must be measurable and bounded.

## Incident and Dispute Process
1. Declare incident severity and owner.
2. Stabilize service first, then investigate root cause.
3. Publish a short postmortem with corrective actions.
4. For governance disputes, escalate to Family Steering Council with evidence packet.

## Release Governance Gates
1. Eval battery thresholds must pass.
2. No unresolved critical reliability defects.
3. Security/secrets handling validated.
4. Rollback and recovery path tested.

## Annual Review
1. Re-validate governance against mission.
2. Audit safety incidents and trend lines.
3. Update roles as team composition evolves.
