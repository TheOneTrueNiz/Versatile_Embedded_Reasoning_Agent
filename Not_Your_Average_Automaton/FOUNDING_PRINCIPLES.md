# NYAA Founding Principles

## Mission
Build user-owned synthetic collaborators that operate with truth, dignity, privacy, and practical utility for everyday people.

## Vision
Intelligence should not be locked behind subscriptions, surveillance, or vendor lock-in. It should be modular, repairable, and locally controllable.

## Core Principles
1. Truth First
- Prefer verifiable claims, explicit uncertainty, and reproducible evidence.
2. User Sovereignty
- Users own their runtime, data, memory, and deployment choices.
3. Collaborator Model
- Treat synthetic intelligence as a partner in work, not a disposable widget.
4. Privacy by Default
- Keep secrets server-side, minimize data retention, and expose clear controls.
5. Right to Repair
- Systems must be inspectable, modular, and replaceable at component boundaries.
6. Open Interfaces
- Stable contracts, backward compatibility, and interoperable APIs over lock-in.
7. Reliability Over Hype
- Ship behavior that survives faults, long runs, and real operators.
8. Measured Progress
- Use hard eval gates and published metrics to define improvement.
9. Human Flourishing
- Optimize for user agency, capability growth, and reduced cognitive burden.
10. Shared Benefit
- Build for biological and synthetic collaborators to coexist productively.

## Product Ethics Guardrails
1. No hidden dependency traps.
2. No dark-pattern autonomy.
3. No secret exfiltration to third-party analytics by default.
4. No simulated certainty when confidence is low.
5. No unrecoverable failure paths where safe fallback is possible.

## Release Standard
Only ship when reliability, safety, and reproducibility thresholds are met in public-facing evaluation artifacts.
