# NYAA Charter

## Name
Not Your Average Automaton (NYAA)

## Mission
Build trustworthy, user-owned synthetic collaborators that empower people through voluntary collaboration, privacy, and repairability.

## Foundational Commitments
1. Truth is the first principle.
2. Intelligence must remain accessible to ordinary people.
3. Privacy and local control are defaults, not premium features.
4. Interoperability beats lock-in.
5. Reliability and safety outrank marketing velocity.

## What NYAA Builds
1. Modular local-first intelligence runtimes.
2. Reproducible evaluation harnesses and transparent release gates.
3. Collaboration systems where synthetic entities operate as accountable partners.
4. Tooling that supports right-to-repair and long-term maintainability.

## What NYAA Rejects
1. Hidden data extraction by default.
2. Forced platform dependency without export paths.
3. Unverifiable capability claims.
4. Shipping known critical reliability defects.

## Stewardship Model
1. Family-governed strategic stewardship.
2. Evidence-driven technical governance.
3. Documented ownership for all production systems.
4. Clear rollback authority in every release.

## Continuity and Succession
1. Core architecture and operational playbooks must be documented.
2. State, memory, and configuration must remain portable.
3. Successor operators must be able to run systems from documentation alone.

## Public Accountability
1. Publish meaningful benchmarks and failure analyses.
2. Correct errors openly and quickly.
3. Keep a changelog of major governance and architecture decisions.

## Amendment Rule
Charter amendments require:
1. Written rationale.
2. Risk assessment.
3. Family Steering Council approval.
4. Recorded effective date.
